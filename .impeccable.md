## Design Context

### Users
Mixed audience of indie game players and developer/tech folks. They arrive from GitHub, game directories, social media links, or Reddit posts. Viewing context ranges from casual browsing on phones to sitting at a desktop evaluating whether to clone and try the game.

### Brand Personality
Lonely, mysterious, alien — emphasizing isolation on an icy moon, otherworldly atmosphere, and the strangeness of first contact with alien creatures.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark theme is correct for the subject matter (space, isolation, terminal game). The visual tone should evoke a deep-space terminal interface — cold, vast, with moments of warmth from alien encounters. Anti-references: generic SaaS landing pages, bright/cheerful game sites, overly polished corporate design.

### Design Principles
1. **Atmosphere over decoration** — every visual element should reinforce the feeling of being stranded on an alien moon
2. **Terminal authenticity** — honor the CLI nature of the game without falling into lazy monospace-everything
3. **Contrast through warmth** — the cold isolation should be punctuated by warm moments (amber, creature encounters) that make both feel stronger
4. **Respect the player** — clear information hierarchy, no tricks, let the game speak for itself
